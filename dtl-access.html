Overview

Designed for graceful degradation

When systems experience burst load, congestion or adverse conditions, failures often emerge as timing collapse rather than simple capacity limits. Retries amplify, queues grow unpredictable and critical workflows can stall even when infrastructure remains online.

DTL Access is designed to stabilise timing behaviour across critical execution paths so systems degrade in a controlled and predictable way rather than failing abruptly.

What it is

An operating mode of DTL

DTL Access uses the same external, lightweight deployment model as FoundScript DTL. It sits alongside your stack as an API or sidecar service and can be trialled with clear rollback behaviour and minimal operational risk.

It does not replace security controls and it does not block attacks. It focuses on execution timing behaviour so access remains reliable when conditions become unstable.

Stress behaviour

Common failure modes under load

Retry amplification

  • Retries and backoffs drift out of sync
  • Traffic becomes bursty and self reinforcing
  • Critical paths stall behind tail behaviour

Auth and session cascades

  • Login and token flows become unstable
  • Dependency chains amplify latency tails
  • Temporary loss of access becomes likely

Queue and dependency collapse

  • Queues stop behaving predictably
  • Long tail stalls spread across services
  • Recovery takes longer than expected

DTL Access targets timing behaviour that drives these cascades. It focuses on stability and tail control under stress, preserving access without trading away baseline performance.
