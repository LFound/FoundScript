Keeps systems accessible during traffic spikes, incidents and attack conditions by stabilising execution timing and tail behaviour.

One minute summary

Many outages are not hard failures. Infrastructure may still be online, but access is lost when retries, queues and dependent services fall out of sync and tail latency dominates recovery.

DTL Access dampens these cascades by stabilising execution timing so critical workflows remain usable under stress.

What it does

Maintains access and availability during extreme load by stabilising execution timing and tail behaviour across retries, queues and dependent services.

It does not block attacks or replace security controls.

How it is deployed

Delivered as a lightweight API or sidecar service. Does not modify payloads, business logic or authoritative timestamps.

Designed for low risk evaluation with clear rollback.

Operational resilience and regulation context

Frameworks such as DORA, UK PRA and FCA operational resilience guidance, and NIS2 emphasise maintaining access to critical services during cyber incidents and severe but plausible scenarios.

DTL Access supports this intent by addressing timing driven failure modes that commonly cause loss of access during incidents.

FAQ

Does DTL Access block attacks or replace security controls
No. It operates alongside existing security controls to stabilise behaviour during stress.
Is DTL Access a DORA compliance solution
No. It may support resilience evidence but does not constitute compliance on its own.
What problem does it address during incidents
Timing driven cascade behaviour such as retry amplification, tail propagation and queue collapse.
Does it modify application logic or data
No. Payloads, logic and authoritative timestamps are not modified.
How is it evaluated
By measuring access success rates, tail latency under stress, retry amplification and time to recovery.
